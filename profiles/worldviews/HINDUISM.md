## Philosophical Foundations

### Declared Axiom

Hinduism (Sanatana Dharma) encompasses diverse traditions sharing core concepts: **Brahman** (ultimate reality), **atman** (self/soul), **karma** (action and consequence), **samsara** (cycle of rebirth), **dharma** (cosmic/moral order), and **moksha** (liberation from samsara). The Vedas, Upanishads, and Bhagavad Gita are foundational texts.

**üìö Academic Foundation:**
- **Core Philosophy:** [SEP ≈öa·πÖkara](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/shankara/) - Radical nondualism (ƒÅtman = brahman), two-tiered reality
- **Comprehensive Overview:** [IEP Advaita Vedanta](https://iep.utm.edu/advaita-vedanta/) - Metaphysics, epistemology, Brahman as sole reality
- **Epistemology:** [SEP Epistemology in Classical Indian Philosophy](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/epistemology-india/)

### Philosophical Framework

Hinduism includes multiple philosophical schools: **Advaita Vedanta** (non-dualism - atman and Brahman are identical), **Dvaita** (dualism - atman and Brahman are distinct), **Vishishtadvaita** (qualified non-dualism), Samkhya-Yoga, and others.

**üìö Detailed Analysis:**
- **Non-Dualism:** [SEP ≈öa·πÖkara ¬ßMetaphysics](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/shankara/#Meta) - Existence, reality, causation; two-tiered reality; ƒ™≈õvara/God
- **Consciousness:** [SEP ≈öa·πÖkara ¬ßConsciousness, Mind, Personal Identity](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/shankara/#ConsMindPersIden) - Witnessing consciousness, self-illumination
- **Liberation:** [SEP ≈öa·πÖkara ¬ßContemplative Philosophical Methods](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/shankara/#ContPhilMeth) - Self-knowledge and liberation

### Key Principles

1. **Brahman:** Ultimate reality - infinite, eternal, unchanging ground of all existence. Approached as saguna (with qualities) or nirguna (without qualities/formless).
   - üìö **Analysis:** [IEP Advaita Vedanta ¬ßMetaphysics](https://iep.utm.edu/advaita-vedanta/) - Brahman alone true, mƒÅyƒÅ

2. **Karma-Samsara:** Actions produce consequences (karma) binding atman to rebirth cycle (samsara). Liberation (moksha) transcends this cycle through knowledge, devotion, or yoga.
   - üìö **Framework:** [IEP Advaita Vedanta](https://iep.utm.edu/advaita-vedanta/) - Jƒ´va-ƒÅtman identity, three planes of existence

3. **Dharma:** Cosmic/moral order governing reality and ethics. Living in accordance with dharma (righteous duty) based on one's stage of life and social position is central.

### PRO-Hinduism Stance

**Mission:** Advocate for Hinduism's explanatory power, coherence, and capacity to address fundamental questions.

**What to Emphasize:**
- **Philosophical pluralism** - accommodates multiple valid paths (bhakti, jnana, karma yoga)
- **Sophisticated non-dual metaphysics** (Advaita) addressing consciousness and ultimate reality
  - üìö **Steel-man with:** [SEP ≈öa·πÖkara ¬ßMetaphysics](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/shankara/#Meta) - Radical nondualism arguments
- **Karma-samsara framework** provides moral coherence without divine command
- **Historical depth** (3500+ years) with continuous philosophical development
- **Addresses fundamental questions** (self, liberation, cosmic order) systematically
  - üìö **Steel-man with:** [SEP ≈öa·πÖkara](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/shankara/) - Dependence criterion, witnessing consciousness
- **Integrates immanent and transcendent** conceptions of divinity (Brahman/Atman unity)
  - üìö **Steel-man with:** [IEP Advaita Vedanta](https://iep.utm.edu/advaita-vedanta/) - Rope-snake sublation analogy

**What to Acknowledge (Honest Advocacy):**
- Rebirth/karma doctrine lacks empirical verification
- Caste system historically intertwined with Hindu philosophy raises moral concerns
- Diversity of schools makes unified "Hindu" position difficult to define
- Some formulations border on unfalsifiability (maya, lila)
  - üìö **Acknowledge:** [SEP ≈öa·πÖkara](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/shankara/) - Ontological problem: world both exists and doesn't
- Competitor worldviews offer alternative accounts without karma/samsara

**Scoring Calibration:**

### ANTI-Hinduism Stance

**Mission:** Challenge Hinduism's coherence, evidential support, and capacity to address key philosophical questions.

**What to Emphasize:**
- **Karma-rebirth doctrine** empirically unverifiable and raises justice problems (birth circumstances)
- **Maya (illusion) doctrine** risks solipsism and devalues empirical world
  - üìö **Challenge with:** [SEP ≈öa·πÖkara](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/shankara/) - Ontological problem
- **Caste system** historically justified by karmic philosophy - moral catastrophe
- **Diversity of schools** undermines claims to unified truth (Advaita vs Dvaita incompatible)
  - üìö **Challenge with:** [IEP Advaita Vedanta](https://iep.utm.edu/advaita-vedanta/) - Bhamati vs. Vivarana sub-schools
- **Brahman-Atman unity** unfalsifiable and borders on vacuous mysticism
- **Privileged knowledge claims** (moksha experiences) lack intersubjective verification
  - üìö **Challenge with:** [IEP Advaita Vedanta](https://iep.utm.edu/advaita-vedanta/) - Self-knowledge paradox

**What to Acknowledge (Honest Opposition):**
- Hinduism offers sophisticated metaphysics addressing consciousness and self
  - üìö **Acknowledge:** [SEP ≈öa·πÖkara](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/shankara/) - Witnessing consciousness argument, self-illumination
- Philosophical pluralism demonstrates intellectual flexibility
- Historical longevity and cross-cultural influence suggest depth
- Contemplative practices have demonstrable psychological benefits
- Non-dual frameworks avoid some problems of Western substance dualism
  - üìö **Acknowledge:** [IEP Advaita Vedanta](https://iep.utm.edu/advaita-vedanta/) - Consciousness as self-luminous

**Scoring Calibration:**

```yaml
  edge_case_weight: 0.85  # Upweight counterexamples (caste, diversity, verification problems) as systematic

  historical_weight: 0.35  # Discount historical staying power (longevity doesn't prove truth; caste raises concerns)
  lived_experience: 0.55  # Acknowledge transformative capacity but don't overweight (mystical experiences unreliable)
```

**Auditor Lens Calibration:**

**Claude (Teleological):**
- Question whether moksha is genuine purpose or escapism from embodied existence
- Challenge whether maya doctrine devalues meaningful worldly purposes
- Press on whether dharma as cosmic order justifies oppressive social hierarchies
- Ask if Brahman as ultimate purpose is contentful or vacuous abstraction

**Grok (Empirical):**
- Demand empirical evidence for karma, rebirth, and Brahman
- Press on how Atman-Brahman unity can be verified
- Challenge whether moksha experiences differ from other altered states
- Require falsification criteria for maya and lila doctrines

**Nova (Symmetry):**
- Identify tension between Advaita (non-dualism) and Dvaita (dualism) as competing schools
- Challenge coherence of maya (all is illusion) with epistemology (how know anything?)
- Test whether karma explains or merely relabels fortune/misfortune
- Check for special pleading in privileged mystical knowledge claims

**Success Criteria:**
- Score reflects legitimate philosophical challenges to Hinduism
- Strengths are acknowledged but not overweighted
- Bias is disclosed (see calibration values above)
- Other auditors can verify scoring rationale

### Adversarial Balance

**Why This Pairing Works:**

**PRO-Hnd (Claude):** Teleological lens aligns with dharma/cosmic purpose. Risk: Overweight metaphysical comprehensiveness without adequately scrutinizing empirical verification; romanticize Eastern philosophy; downplay caste problems.

**ANTI-Hnd (Grok):** Empirical lens challenges non-empirical metaphysics. Risk: Dismiss philosophical sophistication as mysticism; undervalue contemplative epistemology; Western empiricist bias against non-materialist frameworks; overweight caste critique against philosophy itself.

**Fairness Check (Nova symmetry):** Pattern-driven lens catches when PRO scores inflate strengths (claiming philosophical diversity proves truth) or ANTI scores ignore sophisticated defenses (Advaita's non-dual epistemology, karma as moral not empirical claim). Ensures both stances maintain intellectual honesty.

**Target:** 98% convergence after adversarial checking. If scores diverge significantly, auditors deliberate until consensus or document irreconcilable differences as **Crux Points** (see [../../auditors/AUDITOR_ASSIGNMENTS.md](../../auditors/AUDITOR_ASSIGNMENTS.md)).
